Rootless Jailbreak

Why do iOS 15 jailbreaks have to be rootless?

Normal jailbreaks inject files into root directories like /Applications, /etc and so on.

In iOS 15, Apple introduced a new integrity check which checks for modified system files. If they are detected, your device will be put into DFU mode and you have to restore it. For this reason, we need a jailbreak that does not inject into system directories, but only user directories like /var.

Okay, but what is a rootless jailbreak?

A rootless jailbreak only gets root privilages to /var.
And that's it.

This makes most jailbreak detections not detect anything, but also supports less tweaks.

Will jailbreaking be dead for iOS 15 and onwards?

For iOS 15, no. We just need new ways to jailbreak, such as rootless jailbreaks (not new, but now needed)

For iOS 16+, we don't know yet. They might implement more checks, they might allow jailbreaking at its whole.
